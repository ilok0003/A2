Energy Consumption Worldwide

A visualization representation of energy consumption patterns across different regions of the world.

This choropleth map showcases the energy consumption per capita, adjustable across the years from 1985 to 2021.

This bubble chart represents the relationship between energy use per capita and the share in extreme poverty for the year 2014, with the bubble size indicating population size.

The stacked area chart below depicts per capita energy source by year, categorizing them into fossil, nuclear, and renewables.

The bar chart visualizes opposition to nuclear energy globally, with an average opposition rate of 62%.

This bubble chart illustrates the correlation between per capita CO2 emissions and per capita energy consumption for the year 2021, with bubble sizes corresponding to population figures.
